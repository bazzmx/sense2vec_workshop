Domestic equity markets were trading in red in early trade on Monday following mixed global cues.The 30-share BSE Sensex was down over 50 points at 30517 around 9.20 am (IST), while the NSE Nifty50 index was down 15.70 points, or 0.17 per cent, at 9422.55.Market sentiments dampened after a powerful blast ripped through a crowded arena hosting US star Ariana Grande's pop concert in the English city of Manchester, killing 19 people and injuring about 50 others, in one of the deadliest terror attacks in the UK.Among the 51 components in the Nifty index, 31 were trading in red with Gail falling the most 4.20 per cent followed by Sun Pharmaceuticals (down 3.87 per cent), Aurobindo Pharma (down 2.96 per cent), Cipla (down 2.01 per cent) and Tata Power (down 1.58 per cent).State-owned gas utility GAIL India on Monday reported 69 per cent drop in fourth quarter net profit as it wrote down the value of its investment in Dabhol power plant.Net profit for the January-March quarter of the last fiscal was Rs 260.16 crore, or Rs 1.54 per share, as against Rs 832.13 crore, or Rs 4.92 a share, net earnings in the same period a year before.On the other hand, HCL Technologies Asian Paints and Maruti Suzuki were trading higher by 1.22 per cent, 1.18 per cent, 1.01 per cent, 0.84 per cent and 0.77 per cent, respectively.Foreign portfolio investors bought shares worth Rs 3,172.65 crore in domestic equity markets on Tuesday with gross purchases and gross sales of Rs 8,772.35 crore and Rs 5,599.70 crore, respectively.Among the sectoral indices on BSE, the BSE Healthcare index was trading down by 2.33 per cent in early trade, followed by Realty (down 1.70 per cent) and Consumer Durables (down 1.37 per cent). On the other hand, BSE IT and BSE TECk index were up by 0.65 per cent and 0.41 per cent, respectively.Government bonds rose on Monday, as comments from some government officials that implementation of the proposed Goods and Services Tax (GST) will further ease inflation boosted hopes of a less-hawkish monetary policy outlook next month.The benchmark 6.97% 2026 bond yield fell to 6.67 per cent on Monday from 6.71 per cent in the previous session.
