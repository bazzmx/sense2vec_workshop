It was a positive week for domestic equity markets. During the week, market sentiment remained positive on hopes of a possible interest rate cut by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) after the latest data showed a sharp fall in headline retail inflation. Meanwhile, reports suggested that monsoon is expected to arrive on the southern Kerala coast two days ahead of schedule. Good monsoon rains will further ease inflation going forward. Goods and Services Tax (GST) Council finalising GST rates for the goods and services further aided sentiment.For the week ended May 19, the BSE Sensex advanced 276.77 points or 0.91 per cent to settle at 30,464.92, while the Nifty50 index gained 27 points or 0.28 per cent to settle at 9,427.90.Among the top gainers in the NSE Nifty index, Tata Steel surged 12.09 per cent in the last five trading sessions, followed by TCS (up 6.21 per cent), Lupin (up 5.12 per cent), ITC (up 4.09 per cent) and ICICI Bank (up 3.54 per cent).On the other hand, Tech Mahindra BPCL and YES Bank declined 5.90 per cent, 4.55 per cent, 4.26 per cent, 4.19 per cent and 3.57 per cent, respectively.In addition to this, below are some of the sectors and stocks which hogged limelight during the week. Have a look: Shares of Avanti Feeds surged 56 per cent in five trading session. The company on last Saturday reported 168.59 per cent rise in net profit at Rs 83.88 crore for the quarter ended March 31, 2016. It had posted net profit of Rs 31.23 crore in the corresponding quarter last year. The scrip surged from Rs 898.30 on last Friday to Rs 1403.15 on May 19.Shares of Tata Steel surged over 12 per cent this week as the company’s consolidated net loss narrowed to Rs 1,168.02 crore for the quarter ended March 31, 2017. It had posted a consolidated net loss of Rs 3,041.88 crore for the year-ago quarter. The scrip jumped from Rs 436.75 on May 12, 2017 to Rs 489.40 on May 19.Shares of Idea Cellular dropped nearly 5 per cent for the week ended May 19 after the telecom major on Monday reported a consolidated loss of Rs 325.6 crore for the March quarter. The telecom operator had reported a loss of Rs 384 crore in the December quarter and a profit of 451.70 crore in the corresponding quarter last year. The scrip plunged from Rs 92.30 on May 12 to Rs 87.90 on May 19.Shares of country’s largest lender State Bank of India (SBI) advanced 1.72 per cent to Rs 308.15 on Friday after net profit of bank more than doubled to Rs 2,814.82 crore for the March quarter as its net non-performing assets (NPA) or bad loans narrowed to 3.7 per cent of total advances. The state-owned bank had in contrast registered standalone net profit of Rs 1,263.81 crore in the fourth quarter of the previous fiscal, 2015-16.Shares of FMCG companies hogged limelight on Friday as common use products such as hair oil, soaps and toothpaste will cost less from July 1 when GST is due to set in. The BSE FMCG index jumped 1.86 per cent to 9627.43 on Friday with Colgate Palmolive , ITC, Tata Coffee, Emami, HUL and Jay Shree Tea gaining up to 4 per cent.IDBI Bank’s stock fell nearly 9 per cent this week as the bank on Thursday announced a historic loss of Rs 3,200 crore in four quarter ended March 31, 2017. The lender had posted a net loss of Rs 1,735.81 crore in the corresponding quarter last year.Shares of Dilip Buildcon surged over 6 per cent in the past five trading session. The scrip jumped from Rs 446.65 on May 12 to Rs 475.25 on May 19. The scrip also scaled new all-time high of Rs 529.45 on Thursday after the company on Wednesday reported 222.47 per cent rise in net profit at Rs 195.77 crore for the quarter ended March 31, 2017 against Rs 60.71 crore in the corresponding quarter last year.Shares CESC tanked over 10 this week after RP Sanjiv Goenka Group announced restructuring of its flagship company CESC Ltd by splitting it into four companies. Group chairman Sanjiv Goenka said CESC is being spilt into four companies by way of 'mirror image' demerger for value unlocking and focused management into each of the verticals. For the week ended March 19, share price of the company closed 10.20 per cent down at Rs 877.80 against Rs 977.55 on May 12.Shares of Punjab National Bank slipped nearly 8 per cent this week despite the lender on Tuesday reported a profit after tax (PAT) of Rs 261.90 crore for the March quarter. The bank had reported a loss of Rs 5,367 crore in the corresponding quarter of last year.
